Conclusion

Ultimately, while Bushfires have gained widespread notoriety in recent times, Australia's other natural disasters are still very serious. Heatwaves/Droughts have historically been the deadliest, lasting the longest and killing the most number of people. In terms of seasonal disasters, floods/storms occur in high frequency and cause the most amount of financial, building and vehicle damage.
